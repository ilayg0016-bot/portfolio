I


‍ If it was another’s mouth
If it was another’s eyes
Would you have trusted them more?
Would you once more have called it a disguise?

Was it me who did nothing
To stop it or allow
Is fault here mine for hoping
That to a simple word, he would bow?

Am I the vicious threat
For trusting even now
That should not be who to fret
My lover, the one to which i vow?

What could his have told you
To make you forget just what you saw
Dissuade you from holding him accountable to
Exploiting my form raw

That my mouth simply couldn’t come to wish
To think to know to say
How big of a facade has he lowered, how lish
To keep his crimes at bay?

If it was another’s mouth
If it was another’s eyes
Would you have trusted those tears, guided them to drought
Would you have called their scars too a disguise?

Because now I look up at the skies
I know just what he did
And the sorrow not lone comes from his lies
The fog and I remember too, what you hid

What you chose to stand behind
I know what you’re willing to do to keep closed, the lid
Not to pandora’s box not to the extremes of mind
But to harm inflicted upon a soul you wished so much to of get rid

II

If it was another’s mouth
If it was another’s eyes
That I kissed, that I looked into, what would these words be about?
Was he the choice I made wrong, am I guilty for not I was wise

Can you blame me for how this situation came to arise,
Can you, me, for my naive emotional ties, despise?

Because you weren’t around for the part where it happened
You weren’t by my side when I said no, when he would snap and
You weren’t there, I cant describe the despair
That his pair, of eyes, would undergo, oh how they were just so saddened

When my stream of thought was interrupted by demands so sudden
And I was alone with him in an empty house
As his eyes, they browsed around, browsed around for my blouse to unbutton
Before I could explain that not my intention was to arouse

You haven’t seen the wretched stares of this glutton
Oh how he made me feel like everything else was just a drowse

III

If it was another’s mouth
If it was another’s eyes
I told you he kissed, looked into lovingly
Would you see me as the cat or as one of the mice

If I was betrayed another way,
If his company of another was to be our demise
Would you choose different things to say
Would I be right then, would for I then be more wise?

If still not would you see, just the person he came to be
If this betrayal would be another one to prove just out of reach
Would then again you let him explain, his case plea
Would of contract be punished his careless rash breach?

If it wasnt my home, where it happened
If not in mine but another’s house
His eyes, they browsed around,
Browsed around for a blouse to unbutton
Would you believe that it wasnt his intention to arouse
If he claimed it had occurred just so sudden

Would you see that as a just and right cause
What if there was an inexplicable despair,
That his pair, of eyes underwent, if they looked so saddened
Would I again be expected to treat everything as if it were a mere drowse?

IV

If it was another’s mouth
If it was another’s eyes
That stared at me with that gluttony, if his were the words they spout
Then those rare moments of kindness wouldn’t have felt like such a prize

With him, I could do nothing
But to suck it up and allow
If that is what you call hoping
That to a simple word, he would bow

I said no, I promise im not lying
That may be the only thing about this to which I can avow
But as his reactions were intensifying
To stop it, I couldn’t see how

He is the worst kind of threat
The kind im afraid I would trust even now
For so long his words I have fret
Fret my trust in ones to which i vow

I know why you believe whatever his told you
Why you would pretend to forget just what you saw
I know for I have fallen for his words too
And once upon a time, for them my blood I would draw

But I just cant even bring myself to wish
To understand how you could make another pay
How big of a facade you could partake in, just how lish
To keep his crimes to another at bay?

V

For it was another’s mouth
It was another’s eyes
Another’s trust he had exploited
How could you not him, despise?

How could you do nothing
Not stop it and allow
Blame someone for hoping
That to a simple word, he would bow?

Pose them as some vicious threat
How could you disavow
Words of a victim, and the perpetrator abet
How could you yourself to such a person vow?

What could his have told you
To make you ignore just what you saw
Dissuade you from holding him accountable to
Exploiting my form raw

That my mouth simply couldn’t come to wish
To think to know to say
How big of a facade has he lowered, how lish
To keep his crimes at bay?

Or did he not have to, was it that unimportant
Tell me, to you, was it just another day?
Then of course, you can claim this all to be a lie at the very end, and only I will pay
