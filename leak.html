I


‍ She writes stories of another mouth
Living a life so sore
It screams it lies and then comes that pout
Hitting a lonesome shore

Of new, of what above it already knew
The departure of a warmness
Replaced by a shore possessing so few

Maybe it wished for a warning
Perhaps that would be of all it asks
But it only knew the kisses, the warming
And the cruel offensive tasks

Unaware of what has come,
Unaware of who was to go,
It only knew to smile and hum
Not to reap, and not to sow

Though it had not the brains above
Though it might have lacked a form aware
It wasnt unaffected by the fading of this love
This torturous abandonment to bear

It noticed a lack of touch to sedate
Though it was barely a difference, no scar this new tore
But the oceans here, it noticed, are not so great
They leak like none before

Saltier, they taste of a wait
Of concealer that she wore
They taste of the weight
Of feeling like such a bore

Then exposed is her wretched truth
Living a life so sore
Under all the might all the ruth
Miserable down to core

The mouth it licks and tastes
Sour sad remains
Of such loves such hastes,
Worlds in which it seldom rains

But now the shore no longer bares
A lover, who cares
And more importantly
No one stays up with her, when she gets nightmares

And once again as she has many times before
She lies there, in acceptance of her lonesome shore
This isn’t grief
This is a lack of another soul to bore

A lack of an observer
Not that she would ever dare to ask of life another mouth
After all she was barely granted this fervour
This chance to know who she’s supposed to be about

Who she’s supposed to hate
Who she’s supposed to adore
But the oceans here are not so great
They leak like none before
